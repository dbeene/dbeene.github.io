DANIEL BEENE

Postdoctoral Fellow Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health
Affiliate Member: NM INSPIRES | UNM METALS Superfund Research Program | Center for Native Environmental Health Equity Research
I'm a geographer and postdoctoral fellow at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health in the Department of Epidemiology, where I work with the Environmental influences on Child Health Outcomes (ECHO) Data Analysis Center (DAC). I study the ways that GIScience and geospatial data converge with transdisciplinary efforts to understand and address cross-cutting health concerns in the US and worldwide. I advocate for the critical evalutaion of geospaial data as a fundamental part of the data lifecycle, and, using mixed-methods approaches, I specifically focus on the potential for environmental and social justice to inform and transform geospatial data science.
